About the University

Maharshi Dayanand Saraswati University (MDSU) is a public affiliating university established in 1987 under the Rajasthan University Act. Located in Ajmer — a city of historical and cultural significance — the university serves as the apex academic body for higher education across the Ajmer, Bhilwara, Nagaur, and Tonk districts of Rajasthan.

The university takes its name from Maharshi Dayanand Saraswati (1824–1883), the Sanskrit scholar and social reformer who founded the Arya Samaj movement. His philosophy — grounded in the Vedas yet committed to rational enquiry and social reform — continues to shape the university's academic identity.

MDSU offers postgraduate and doctoral programmes across sciences, arts, commerce, law, education, management, and professional disciplines. Through its network of over 600 affiliated colleges, it shapes the academic futures of hundreds of thousands of students each year.

"Back to the Vedas" — not as retreat, but as return to first principles: reason, inquiry, and universal brotherhood.
— Maharshi Dayanand Saraswati (1824–1883)

Vision & Mission

Vision

A Centre of Excellence in Knowledge and Values

To be a leading university that fosters academic excellence, research, and innovation while upholding the values of equity, integrity, and service to society — rooted in India's intellectual heritage and engaged with the world.

Mission

Accessible, Quality Education for All

To provide quality higher education accessible to all sections of society; to promote research and scholarly inquiry; to develop graduates who are intellectually capable, ethically grounded, and socially responsible.

Administration

The university is governed by its Board of Management, Academic Council, and Finance Committee, operating under the Rajasthan Universities Act. The Vice Chancellor is the principal executive officer responsible for the administration and academic leadership of the institution.

Campus & Facilities

The MDSU campus spans a substantial area on the outskirts of Ajmer city, housing academic departments, administrative offices, residential hostels, a central library, sports facilities, and health care services.

Central Library

The central library holds an extensive collection of books, journals, and digital resources across disciplines. It provides access to UGC-INFLIBNET e-resources, enabling students and faculty to access thousands of academic journals and databases.

Research & Innovation

The university has established a dedicated Innovation Cell and multiple Centres of Excellence (Shodhpeeths) to foster research culture, industry collaboration, and entrepreneurship among students and faculty. The recently inaugurated Music Research Centre and Musical Instruments Museum showcases the university's commitment to preserving India's cultural heritage.

Student Life

MDSU offers residential hostels for male and female students, a health care centre, sports facilities, cultural committee, cafeteria, bank, and post office — making it a self-contained academic campus.
